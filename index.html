About Me

I started programming during COVID because I wanted to understand how things really work behind the scenes. The first time a project actually worked, I knew this was something I wanted to pursue seriously.
I focus on front-end development, creating clean, functional interfaces, and I take pride in building projects from start to finish. One of my favorite achievements was creating a Cybersecurity Challenge Platform for our school project For The Root with my friend Hugo Batoon, inspired by OverTheWire — it challenged us to think creatively, solve complex problems, and collaborate effectively, and seeing it work was incredibly rewarding.
When I hit a roadblock, I don’t give up — I research, debug, and refine until everything works as it should. Outside of coding, I keep learning through tutorials, documentation, and even logical gaming, always sharpening my problem-solving skills.
In the next few years, my goal is to grow into a full-stack developer, building complete applications while continuously improving my skills and pushing my limits.
